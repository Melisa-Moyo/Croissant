Croissant

What is a Croissant?
A croissant is a laminated, yeast-leavened bakery product that contains dough/roll-in fat layers to create a flaky, crispy texture. Croissants belong to the Viennoiserie or pastry category of baked goods along with brioche, Danish and puff pastries. A croissant usually contains normal levels of salt, yeast and sugar.croissant is more than a mere pastry; it's a masterpiece that bridges the gap between baking and artistry.

Origin
The croissant's origin dates back to Vienna in 1683. Legend has it that it was created to commemorate the city's defense against Turkish troops. The Viennese bakers, alerted by the attack, crafted a crescent-shaped pastry symbolizing the Ottoman emblem. Later introduced to France, Marie-Antoinette popularized the croissant in the late 1700s, adding to its global fame.

Types of Croissant
Croissants come in various delightful variations that cater to different tastes and preferences. The classic Plain Croissant stands as a testament to simplicity and indulgence, boasting layers of tender dough with a delicate balance of buttery richness. For those seeking a touch of sweetness, the Almond Croissant emerges as a popular choice, enveloping almond paste within its layers and adorned with a sprinkle of sliced almonds, offering a delightful nutty contrast. A delectable twist on tradition can be found in the Chocolate Croissant, where ribbons of chocolate intermingle with each fold of the dough, creating a luscious melange of flavors. For savory enthusiasts, the Ham and Cheese Croissant combines the flakiness of croissant dough with the salty goodness of ham and the gooey allure of melted cheese, resulting in a satisfying and savory experience.
